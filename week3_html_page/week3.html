Theory: Professional video production

Professional video production has three phases:

  • Pre-production: Planning details, making a script
  • Production: Shooting and recording audio
  • Post-production: Editing the video

Describe in your own words what are following:

  • Synopsis: A synopsis is a short description of what happens in the film. It’s the idea for the film, put into words.
  • Shooting script: A script is the blueprint and foundation for the video. Every film or TV broadcast is based on a script.
  • Storyboard: A storyboard is similar to a comic strip: each scene is drawn shot by shot, showing the camera angles and framings to use. (the rule of thirds, composition, axis of action, film structure, etc.)

Problems if planning is not done well and into detail in pre-production:

  • plot can be not logical
  • story keeps jumping and make no sense
  • waste of time

When creating video for ”normal” web use:

  • most common video aspect ration is 16:9
  • Nowadays 1080p is enough almost for any internet use (1920x1080 pixels)
  • 24 fps – common video standards
